Foreword about my interests and expertise

I am a metamorphic petrologist studying the implications of converging tectonic plates. My work involves characterizing and analyzing metamorphic rocks from micro- to macro-scales to quantify how metamorphic reactions effectively regulate geodynamic features in subduction zones and orogenic belts. I approach geodynamic problems with a broad skillset, including: carefully studying rocks in the field, experimenting with numerical models, training machines to analyze geophysical datasets with geostatistical techniques, applying well-established petrologic methods (e.g. thermodynamic modeling) to various rock types, and advancing cutting-edge empirical and computational models of metamorphic processes (e.g. elastic barometry & generative sampling). Thus, my students might visit remote mountains to sample rocks, use analytical instruments in laboratories, conduct numerical experiments on high-performance computing systems, and/or write code for research, scientific communication, and education. I also have special interests in scholarly teaching, developing cutting-edge learning materials, and applying machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) techniques to advance the Geosciences.
